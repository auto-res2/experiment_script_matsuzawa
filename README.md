## Abstract
We present Adaptive Multimodal Instruction and Co-Training (AMICT), a unified transformer‐based language model that extends the efficient BTLM-3B-8K architecture while systematically overcoming several of its inherent limitations. The baseline BTLM-3B-8K, trained on 627 billion tokens from the SlimPajama dataset with context lengths of 2,048 and 8,192 tokens, achieved competitive performance relative to larger 7B parameter models through aggressive hyperparameter tuning, the SwiGLU activation function, ALiBi positional embeddings, and maximal update parameterization (\(\mu\)P). Despite its strong performance on benchmarks in common sense reasoning, reading comprehension, world knowledge, and long-context summarization, the baseline is constrained by its exclusive focus on text, limited support for interactive instruction-following, and difficulties in processing multimodal inputs or extended sequences beyond approximately 9,216 tokens. In contrast, AMICT introduces four key innovations that markedly enhance both performance and applicability. First, it employs a dual-stage multimodal pretraining regimen that leverages a curated text-plus-multimodal corpus—combining high-quality text with aligned image snippets and short audio segments—to fuse visual and auditory cues with language data and thereby improve integrated instruction-following. Second, an advanced instruction-tuning and alignment stage is incorporated via a multi-stage post-pretraining fine-tuning process that explicitly teaches the model to generate context-aware responses in interactive scenarios such as chat, query-answering, and visual instruction following. This phase is further refined by a lightweight reinforcement learning loop that utilizes web-based feedback to verify factual accuracy and mitigate hallucinations and biases. Third, to address the challenges of extended context processing, AMICT integrates a dynamic context modulation module that adaptively redistributes attention weights based on the semantic density of the input; this mechanism preserves coherence and factual accuracy even when processing sequences beyond nominal token limits, ensuring a gradual rather than abrupt performance decline. Fourth, a hardware–software co-design strategy is adopted to enable efficient on-device inference. By incorporating quantization-friendly architectural components and implementing a shared resource scheduling mechanism across textual and multimodal modules, AMICT achieves a quality–size ratio competitive with larger 7B parameter systems while substantially reducing memory and computational requirements for deployment on mobile and edge devices. Multimodal instruction-following evaluations using a benchmark of aligned text–image pairs, long-context experiments with varied input lengths (e.g., 2K, 8K, and extended sequences), and on-device inference benchmarks with dynamic quantization techniques all confirm the advantages of these innovations. Ablation studies further demonstrate an overall loss reduction of approximately 5.36% relative to the baseline configuration. In summary, by integrating multimodal pretraining, refined instruction-tuning, dynamic long-context handling, and a hardware-aware design for edge deployment, AMICT represents a significant advancement in the development of efficient, contextually aware, and resource-constrained AI systems, thereby laying a solid foundation for future research into enhanced modality integration, improved context modulation techniques, and domain-specific or multilingual applications.
