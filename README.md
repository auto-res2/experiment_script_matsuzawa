## Abstract
\textbf{Abstract}

The transformation of textual prompts into accurate three-dimensional (3D) representations constitutes a significant innovation in both creative and industrial domains. Recent developments have introduced advancements in diffusion models across 2D and 3D paradigms. While 3D diffusion models maintain spatial consistency, their applicability is hindered by limited 3D data availability. Conversely, 2D diffusion models, although adept at rendering details, lack spatial coherence. Addressing these challenges, we propose the Graph-GaussianAssembler framework. This methodology synthesizes the advantages of both paradigms through an innovative Gaussian-based representation, employing a graph neural network for iterative refinement and a joint 2D-3D alignment strategy. Our framework ensures consistency and details while mitigating prevalent issues such as redundancy and artifacts. Comprehensive quantitative evaluations indicate that the Graph-GaussianAssembler excels in geometrical fidelity, visual clarity, and adaptability. This contribution represents a step forward in text-driven 3D asset generation, fostering advancements in real-time visualization and interactive applications.
