## Abstract
Diffusion models have shown significant promise in generative tasks, but their effectiveness relies heavily on the quality of training data. When trained on corrupted or noisy data, these models become vulnerable both to compromised reconstruction fidelity and to malicious backdoor attacks. In this work, we propose Dual-Threshold Consistency and Backdoor Scrubbing (DTCBS), a novel training framework designed to address two major shortcomings of conventional methods. First, DTCBS overcomes the reliance on noisy training data by leveraging a two-stage consistency training scheme based on a double application of Tweedie’s formula. Second, it defends against poisoning attacks by introducing a reverse-noise screening process that identifies samples exhibiting inconsistent denoising behavior across adjacent noise scales. Samples that deviate significantly are flagged via a consistency gate and are either down-weighted or removed during training. Comprehensive experiments on CIFAR-10, using controlled backdoor injections and synthetic noise evaluations, demonstrate that DTCBS not only achieves competitive denoising performance compared to the Base Method but also substantially improves robustness by reducing the presence of backdoor triggers. The proposed approach is validated through rigorous evaluations involving metrics such as mean squared error, PSNR, SSIM, detection rates for poisoned samples, and false positive rates on clean data. Overall, DTCBS provides a mathematically principled and systematically refined training protocol that simultaneously strengthens reconstruction fidelity and enhances adversarial resilience.
